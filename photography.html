Fall 2020

Home Project

The subject of my Photography final project stemmed from what I am thankful for the most. I was inspired by the holidays Thanksgiving and Christmas, a time of thanks and spending time with family. I decided to focus on what makes a house a home and what exemplifies the feeling of being home. My sisters, my mom, my dad, my grandmother, and of course, my dog, Buddy. They are what makes HOME for me.

My sister, Angie, is an incredibly smart student at Northeastern University studying Biomedical Engineering. She was the valedictorian of Haldane High School in 2019. She is an extremely talented pianist and violin player. Since I have completed this project in 2020, Angie has graduated, summa cum laude, from Northeastern University.

My sister, Catriona, is a political science major at Catholic University, studying world politics. She plays the concertina and has competed multiple times at the All Ireland Championships known as the Fleadh Cheoil Irish music festival. Since I have completed this project in 2020, Catriona has graduated, magna cum laude, from Catholic University.


My dad is a computer engineer for IBM. In his free time he loves to cook and watch movies. He is also a musician. He plays the accordion and drums. My mom is an accountant for Deloitte. She loves playing Scrabble and trivia games in her free time. She is also passionate about dancing and singing.

My grandmother (Nanny) hates being photographed, but I was able to sneak a photo of her with my mom playing trivia. Nanny was born in County Mayo, Ireland and moved here at a very young age. She passed down her passion for Irish music and dance to her whole family.

Buddy passed away a year after I completed this project. He lived a full and happy life, and he was the best dog ever.
